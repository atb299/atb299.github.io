About Me

Research Scientist & Modeller

I am a research scientist in the Ocean Modelling group at the National Oceanography Centre (NOC), Southampton. My scientific journey focuses on improving our understanding of the mean state and variability of the ocean and climate system. My research interests include large-scale ocean dynamics, notably the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC), ocean heat content and transport. I participated in the research cruise DY204 in February 2026, which featured on the Channel 4 news.

I employ state-of-the-art numerical ocean general circulation models (OGCMs) and comprehensive observational datasets to analyse global climate. A significant part of my work involves configuring and developing models, with a strong interest in understanding the structural biases, parameters, errors, and uncertainties associated with numerical simulations.

Areas of Research

Core Scientific Focus

Ocean Circulation

Investigating large-scale ocean dynamics, Atlantic meridional transport, and high-frequency wave structures in numerical model configurations and mooring records.

RAPID-AMOC: Analysis of North Atlantic overturning variability and heat transport in support of the transatlantic monitoring array.
Near Inertial Waves (NIWs): Researching superinertial ocean variability and resonances in high-resolution models.
Equatorial Waves: Studying wind-driven oscillations and trapped planetary waves near the equator.

Ocean & Climate Modelling

Configuring, running, and analyzing complex numerical ocean and coupled Earth system configurations to simulate global ocean dynamics.

NEMO: Core user and developer of global configurations of the NEMO ocean modelling framework.
JMMP: Developing global Configurations of the ocean and sea-ice in collaboration with Met Office and NERC.
FORTE 2.0: Publicly available coupled climate model (IGCM4 atmosphere + MOMA ocean) designed for fast, long climate integrations.

Error & Uncertainty

Quantifying parameter space and investigating structural biases in numerical models to improve the reliability of climate projections.

Parametric Tuning: History matching to tune global configurations without over-tuning, using statistical emulators.
Structural Bias: Detecting and mitigating model discrepancies by contrasting perturbed physics ensembles against observations.

The Climate System

Studying multi-scale interactions in climate variability, ranging from short-term wave oscillations to decadal warming trends.

Deep Ocean Heat: Assessing uncertainties in deep ocean temperature trends (2000m+) derived from hydrographic sections.
Warming Hiatus: Modeling ocean heat uptake redistribution during atmospheric warming slowdown events.
Water Cycle Change: Linking ocean warming and salinity fields to trace global water cycle variations.
